Four-plus years herding a protocol nobody owns.

Ethereum doesn't update itself. Network upgrades happen because a lot of
independent people — client teams, researchers, EIP authors — get
coordinated, and the Cat Herders exist to do the coordinating. I started
as a Wrangler in early 2019: occasional note-taker for the All Core Devs
call, EIP shepherding, identifying ecosystem
needs, and lending code where it helped.

Later, as Catblazer, the role turned outward — a self-defined ops
position hunting for coordination problems across the ecosystem and
beyond: dev onboarding, sentiment gathering, initiative discovery. In
2021 that included running the Cat Herders podcast end to end — finding
guests, prepping, interviewing, publishing — with guests like samczsun, Lefteris Karapetsas, Taylor Monahan, and
Tomasz Stańczak.

As one of the original and active members, I was also a long-standing signer on the multisig.
